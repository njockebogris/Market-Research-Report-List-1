<p><p>The composite materials used in wind blades primarily include glass fiber and carbon fiber. Glass fiber materials are cost-effective and widely used due to their good strength-to-weight ratio and durability. They offer flexibility in design and are resistant to corrosion. On the other hand, carbon fiber materials provide superior strength and stiffness, lightweight characteristics, and enhanced fatigue resistance, making them ideal for high-performance applications. The choice between these materials significantly impacts the efficiency, lifespan, and overall performance of wind turbine blades.</p></p>
