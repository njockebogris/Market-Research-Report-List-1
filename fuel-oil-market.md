<p><p>Fuel oil is a refined petroleum product primarily used for heating, power generation, and as a marine fuel. It is categorized into two main types: heavy fuel oil (HFO) and light fuel oil (LFO), with HFO being thicker and requiring preheating for use. This growth is driven by increasing demand from the shipping industry, where fuel oil remains a dominant energy source despite stricter environmental regulations. The rise in global trade and the expansion of the marine sector are key contributors to this demand. Additionally, fuel oil continues to play a vital role in power generation, especially in developing regions with limited access to alternative energy sources.</p><p>Recent trends in the fuel oil market include a shift toward cleaner and low-sulfur variants to comply with environmental standards, such as the International Maritime Organization's (IMO) 2020 sulfur cap. This has led to increased adoption of very low sulfur fuel oil (VLSFO) and investments in refining technologies. However, the market faces challenges from the growing adoption of renewable energy and natural gas, which are gradually replacing fuel oil in certain applications. Despite this, the market is expected to remain resilient due to its critical role in industries and transportation.</p></p>
